# HelmOS Brand Guidelines

This document is the permanent reference for what HelmOS *is*, as a brand — not a feature list, a design token sheet (see `DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`), or a logo spec (see `LOGO_USAGE.md`). Read this before writing copy, designing a new surface, or making a call about whether something "feels like HelmOS."

## What HelmOS is not

- Not a SaaS product with a pricing page and a sales funnel.
- Not another productivity app competing for a slot next to five others.
- Not a Notion clone, a dashboard template, or a "workspace tool."

## What HelmOS is

**HelmOS is a personal operating system** — the permanent home for every part of one person's life: personal life, work, university, a family business, finances, vehicles, travel, documents, health, and modules that don't exist yet. It starts as a single person's private system and grows, deliberately and carefully, into something trusted family members can share parts of without ever losing what's theirs alone.

## Mission

Give one person a single, calm place to run their entire life from — private by default, organized without effort, and built to last for years rather than to be replaced by next year's trend.

## Vision

A person's HelmOS account becomes the thing they open first, not last — the operating system underneath everything else they do, extensible enough to absorb new parts of life as they show up, and trustworthy enough that the people closest to them want to run parts of *their* lives through it too.

## Product personality

If HelmOS were a person, it would be the calm, competent one in the room — the engineer who's already thought about the edge cases, not the one who's still pitching you. Precise without being cold. Confident without needing to prove it. It doesn't chase trends, doesn't over-explain itself, and doesn't need fifteen colors and three gradients to feel alive.

**Three words that describe it:** Composed. Deliberate. Enduring.

## Voice

- **Direct, not clever.** Say what something does. Don't reach for a pun to prove personality (the one exception is the product name itself — "take the helm" — which is earned because it's literally what the product is called, not a running theme).
- **Confident, not hyped.** No exclamation points doing the enthusiasm for you. No "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "supercharged." State the value plainly and let it land.
- **Calm about the future.** Features that don't exist yet (family accounts, AI, new modules) are described as inevitabilities in progress, not upsells or teasers. "Coming" — not "Coming soon!! 🚀"
- **Precise about privacy.** This is the one place voice gets to be a little more emphatic, because it's the one place trust is actually being asked for. "Your data belongs to you" is a claim, not a slogan — back it with specifics, not adjectives.

## Design principles

1. **Calm over clever.** If an interaction needs a tooltip to explain why it's delightful, it isn't.
2. **Timeless over trendy.** Favor decisions that will look correct in five years — flat color, real typography, restrained motion — over whatever's currently popular in dashboard design.
3. **Honest surfaces.** Product previews show the actual product (real components, real tokens), not stock illustrations standing in for it.
4. **Structure is the aesthetic.** The hexagon mark, the grid, the spacing scale — precision and structure aren't decoration here, they're the point, because this is infrastructure for a person's whole life.
5. **Earn every pixel of motion.** Animation exists to clarify state changes and add a sense of quality, never to perform energy the product doesn't have.
6. **Private by construction, not by policy.** Every design decision about data and sharing defaults to private; visibility is something a user grants, never something they have to notice and revoke.

## Where to look when in doubt

Linear, Vercel, Arc, Stripe, Apple, Notion — for quality bar, spacing discipline, and restraint. Not for what to copy. HelmOS should never be mistaken for any one of them; it should read as something that's been around as long as the best of them.
